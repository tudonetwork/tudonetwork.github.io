Learn about Natal

Natal was founded by the Portuguese in the 16th century, with the construction of Reis Magos Fort. The fortress, shaped like a five-pointed star, is one of the city's main postcards. In addition to guarding cannons and many stories, unveil a beautiful view of the urban beaches of the potiguar capital.

The most sought after is Ponta Negra, a competitor during the day due to its kiosks; and hot when the sun is part of bringing together some of the best local bars and restaurants. In many of them, the menu is a substitute for the typical delights of regional cuisine based on seafood, sun meat, green beans, milk rice ...

The day starts early in Natal. The first step is to rent a car - preferably a buggy - to get around the capital and the surrounding area.

Heading to the North coast, the highlights are the Genipabu dunes and Jacumã and Pitangui lagoons, where the squibunda and aerobunda are practiced. To the south, the region's peaceful beaches lead to the village of Pipa, 150 miles away.

When it comes to shopping, craft markets around Christmas are pieces of wood, fabric and glass. In the Tourism Center, a mansion where a public chain operates, the highlights are the delicate maze lace embroidery and the colorful ceramic work.
